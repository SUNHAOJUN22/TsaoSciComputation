# Code of Conduct

TsaoSciComputation discussions must remain professional, evidence-based, and safe.

Participants must:

- discuss ideas, code, evidence, and scientific assumptions rather than attacking people;
- distinguish software correctness from scientific validity and clearly state uncertainty and applicability limits;
- protect credentials, licenses, proprietary data, private datasets, and production-control information;
- avoid fabricated evidence, misleading solver claims, harassment, discrimination, intimidation, and disruptive behavior;
- stop and escalate when work touches safety systems, runaway hazards, regulated data, or decisions requiring qualified domain review.

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content and restrict participation when necessary to protect people, data, repository integrity, or scientific trust. Security concerns must follow `SECURITY.md`; ordinary conduct concerns may be raised privately through a non-public contact route on the repository owner's GitHub profile.
